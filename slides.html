Start with a Problem

Fix something annoying

Start with something that bugs you, wastes time, or is easy to forget.

Make something small

Useful beats huge

A small tool that really helps is better than a giant project you never finish.

Example: Dryer alert

A tiny device could feel the dryer shaking and tell me when it stops.

Platforms

What Are Your Options?

Apple

Apps for iPhone and iPad are often made with Swift.

Android

Apps for Android are often made with Kotlin.

Web

Websites use HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

Cross-platform

Tools like React Native and Flutter try to make one app work in more places.

If you want the easiest thing to share, start with the web. If you want a real phone app, there are more steps.

Stores

Putting an App in the Store

$99
Apple developer cost each year
$25
Google Play cost one time
  • Apple makes you pay every year to put apps in the App Store.
  • Google Play has a one-time sign-up cost, but still has rules and checks.
  • Both stores want screenshots, app info, and other details before you can publish.

Current summary slide content prepared on April 14, 2026.

Testing

How Testing Works

Apple

TestFlight lets people try your app before it is in the App Store.

Google

Google has test groups before your app goes public.

Important

For brand-new personal Google Play accounts, you need 12 testers for 14 days before full release.

Testing is where you find the weird bugs, confusing parts, and stuff that did not work the way you thought.

Reality

Things That Can Be Hard

  • Store rules
  • Phone permissions
  • AI code that is close, but not right
  • Apps acting different on different devices
  • Problems you did not think about at first
  • Projects taking longer than you hoped

The goal is not to be perfect. The goal is to keep learning and keep building.
