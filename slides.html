Use AI

Start with a Problem, Not a Product

Fix an annoyance

AI is great when you start with something inconvenient, repetitive, or easy to forget.

Build small first

The answer does not need to be a full app. It could be a tiny website or a script.

Useful beats huge

A tiny tool that helps every week can matter more than a giant unfinished project.

Example: Dryer alert

An ESP32 plus a vibration sensor could detect when the dryer stops and send a notification.

Example: Bills automation

A morning script could read bill emails and enter them into a budgeting app automatically.

Platforms

What Are Your Options?

Apple

Usually built with Swift or SwiftUI.

Android

Usually built with Kotlin or Jetpack Compose.

Web

Built with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

Cross-platform

React Native, Flutter, and PWAs aim to reuse more code.

If you want the easiest thing to share, start on the web. If you need a real phone app, think about whether native or cross-platform makes more sense.

Testing

How Apps Reach Testers First

Apple

TestFlight is used for beta testing with internal and external testers.

Google

Internal, closed, open, and production tracks exist in Play Console.

Important

New personal Google Play accounts need a closed test with at least 12 opted-in testers for 14 days before production release.

Testing is where real life shows up: weird devices, unclear instructions, login issues, permissions, and bugs you did not expect.

Workflow

What Building with AI Actually Looks Like

1

Pick one specific problem.

2

Ask AI for the smallest useful first version.

3

Generate code, layout, or automation logic.

4

Test what breaks, feels awkward, or seems confusing.

5

Revise, repeat, and keep your own judgment in the loop.

AI helps a lot

It can brainstorm, draft code, explain errors, and suggest design directions very quickly.

But it is not magic

You still need patience, testing, decisions, and the ability to notice when something is wrong.

Reality

Hurdles and Unexpected Things

  • Store rules and account verification
  • Permissions and privacy prompts
  • AI giving code that is close, but not quite right
  • Different behavior across devices or browsers
  • Edge cases you did not think about
  • Projects taking longer than the first draft suggests

The goal is not to avoid every hurdle. The goal is to keep learning, adjusting, and shipping useful things anyway.
